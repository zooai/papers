\begin{abstract}
\Zoo{} 3.0 is the \GPU{}-native graduation of the \Zoo{} stack. Every hot-path primitive --- consensus signature aggregation, \BLS{}12-381 verification, \Ringtail{} threshold proofs, \MLDSA{}-65 identity proofs, \textsc{evm} bytecode execution, \AMM{} curve math, \FHE{} ciphertext arithmetic, and Groth16/Halo2 \ZKP{} verification --- runs as deterministic \GPU{} kernels in pure C++ via the \QuasarGPU{} adapter (\textsc{lp-132}) under the \GPU{}-Residency Invariant (\textsc{lp-137}). With this release, \Zoo{} graduates from a \Lux{} L2 application to a sovereign \Lux{} L1 with its own validator set, \Quasar{}-certified consensus, and independent economic security, while continuing to settle and bridge to the broader \Lux{} chain topology (\textsc{lp-134}). This paper documents the 3.0 architecture: the L1 graduation, the \GPU{}-native execution stack, the \Zoo{} \DEX{} (native \AMM{} V2/V3, V4 as a precompile to the \Lux{} \DEX{}), the \Zoo{} AI Desktop application hosting the \Zen{} model family for free local private inference, the \texttt{zoo-bot} pure-Go re-write of the \Hanzo{} OpenClaw bot, the \Zoo{} Bridge multi-ecosystem connector, dual-path privacy via F-Chain \FHE{} (\textsc{lp-013}) and Z-Chain \ZKP{} (\textsc{lp-063}), and the \NFT{} migration program. Spec freeze: 2025-12-15. Activation alongside \Lux{} \Quasar{} 3.0: 2025-12-25.

\textbf{Keywords}: \Zoo{} 3.0, \GPU{}-native, L1 graduation, \Quasar{} 3.0, \Zoo{} \DEX{}, \Zoo{} AI Desktop, \texttt{zoo-bot}, \Zoo{} Bridge, \FHE{}, \ZKP{}, \NFT{} migration
\end{abstract}
