\section{Related Work}

\label{sec:related}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Lux-FHE substrate.} \Zoo{} consumes the \Lux{} FHE stack
unchanged: \texttt{lux/fhe} (Go TFHE on \texttt{luxfi/lattice}~\cite{lux-fhe-go}),
the \texttt{lux/precompile/fhe} native EVM precompiles, and the
\texttt{luxcpp/gpu} dispatcher routing PBS / key-switch /
blind-rotate to backend plugins in
\texttt{lux-private/gpu-kernels/\{cuda,metal,wgsl,mlx\}/fhe/}. The
\Lux{} \texttt{lux-fhe-smart-contracts}~\cite{lux-fhe-smart-contracts}
paper documents the native-precompile architecture (addresses
\texttt{0x...80}--\texttt{0x...83}) that \Zoo{}'s \TChain{} extends
with threshold decryption, \CKKS{} support for approximate arithmetic
(enabling ML inference), and formal verification of noise bounds in
Lean~4.

\textbf{Sunscreen FHE.} The Sunscreen compiler~\cite{sunscreen2023} provides a Rust-based FHE toolkit targeting BFV and CKKS. \Zoo{} builds on similar compiler infrastructure but integrates it directly into the blockchain VM with gas metering and threshold key management.

\textbf{Threshold FHE in MPC.} Boneh et al.~\cite{boneh2018threshold} formalized threshold FHE for multi-party computation. \Zoo{} adapts this framework to the blockchain setting, replacing interactive MPC rounds with asynchronous partial-decryption collection anchored by BFT consensus.
