\begin{abstract}
We introduce \textbf{Proof of AI} (\PoAI{}), a consensus mechanism for networks where
participants contribute AI compute rather than hash power or stake. Bitcoin's hash is
\emph{useless} work with $O(1)$ verification; \PoAI{} replaces it with \emph{useful}
work---genuine AI execution---plus a proof that is a \emph{side effect} of that
execution and cheaply checkable, requiring \emph{software only}: no trusted execution
environment and no GPU attestation. The recomputable ``hash everyone can check''
becomes an activation-trace commitment that cannot be produced without performing the
genuine matrix multiplications. We give one primitive realizing four
execution-level proofs over a workload tag---Proof-of-Embedding, Proof-of-Inference,
Proof-of-Training, and a privacy-preserving Proof-of-Contribution---by committing the
computation trace and spot-checking its matmuls with Freivalds' algorithm over the
int8 path's exact integer accumulator, which yields a bit-exact check with soundness
error $1/p \approx 2^{-61}$ per vector ($2^{-122}$ at $k{=}2$) and \emph{zero} false
rejections. We bind every proof to its task with a domain-separated,
beacon-fresh, measured-model commitment, and prove anti-substitution and anti-replay
for it. We position the scheme as Bitcoin's shared, software-only difficulty---the
Hamiltonian Market Maker \emph{prices} heterogeneous compute while the execution proof
\emph{verifies} it, two orthogonal dials---and give a single profile gate mapping tiers
(zkML, $M$-of-$N$ confidential compute, optimistic-Freivalds, redundancy) to required
proof strength, designed so that once the gate is engaged ``no valid compute proof''
means ``no mint.'' We motivate the
construction with a concrete attack on a deployed settlement engine that today mints
for a one-bit guess, because a signature proves authorship and a plurality proves
agreement, but neither proves computation. We retain \TEE{} attestation, multi-party
quality scoring, and BLS aggregation as the strong and pricing layers; the execution
proofs are what bind reward to genuine work.

\textbf{Status, stated up front.} This paper proves a \emph{primitive}, builds the
\emph{system} around it, and specifies the \emph{residual} that remains design-only; we
mark which is which in the abstract rather than burying it, and
Section~\ref{sec:status-summary} gives the per-component ledger before the construction
begins. What is \emph{built and tested} is now the whole load-bearing path, not just the
core: (i) the exact-integer Freivalds matmul verifier and its keccak-Merkle
activation-trace transcript, a proof-bearing forward (\texttt{ProvableLinear}) whose
returned value \emph{is} the committed exact-integer result, and a typed full-graph trace
(\texttt{GraphTrace}) that binds attention, MoE, and---with one transpose op---a training
step, all in the Rust engine (\texttt{hanzo-engine}: $27$ unit tests $+\,11$ adversarial
red-team tests, $38/38$ passing); (ii) the canonical Go verifier
(\texttt{luxfi/crypto/poi}, $23/23$) that the chain consumes, byte-identical to the
prover under a pinned cross-language challenge vector; and (iii) the on-chain enforcement
(\texttt{luxfi/standard} Proof-of-AI contracts: $87/87$ across the eight Proof-of-AI suites),
where \texttt{ComputeWitnessLib} runs Freivalds and the Merkle fold in pure Solidity,
\texttt{OptimisticEvidence} pays a bond to a challenger \emph{only} on a verified fraud,
and the \texttt{AIMiner} mint gate \emph{fails closed}: with no compute-verifier wired
it reverts (\texttt{ProofGateUnavailable}), so a forged receipt root alone no longer mints.
Two honest properties survive the build: a fabricated matmul is caught except with
probability $\le 2^{-122}$ at two challenge vectors because Freivalds runs over an exact
integer accumulator, and an honest prover is \emph{never} slashable. What remains
\emph{designed, not built} is the genuinely smaller residual: the RepOps pinned-reduction
floating-point tier (the quantized integer tier is what ships), and emission coverage of
architecture families beyond those exercised by the test suite (dense/SwiGLU, attention
with a fixed-point integer softmax, an MoE block, a training step). The honest one-line
summary: \emph{the soundness primitive is proven, the unfakeable-mining system it enables
is built and tested end to end, and the named residual is a floating-point tier and wider
architecture coverage}.

\textbf{The \Zoo{} reading.} \Zoo{} Labs Foundation is the Layer-3 application and research
layer (decentralized AI and decentralized science, governed through \texttt{zips.zoo.ngo})
that \emph{consumes} this stack. Section~\ref{sec:l3-settlement} shows how a
\Zoo{} L3---\Beluga{} and its peers---registers in an on-chain directory
(\texttt{L3Registry}) and settles PoAI-verified work down to the \Zoo{} L2 and the Lux L1,
so that proven inference, embedding, and training become the reproducibility substrate for
decentralized science: a result is not ``trust me, I ran the model'' but a transcript whose
root anyone can open and re-check.

\textbf{Keywords}: consensus, proof of useful work, AI compute verification, Freivalds'
algorithm, optimistic verification, decentralized science, Layer-3 settlement,
post-quantum cryptography, verifiable machine learning
\end{abstract}
